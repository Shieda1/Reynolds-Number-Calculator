What is the reynolds number?
The reynolds number is a factor in the prediction flow patterns in different fluid flow situations by measuring the ratio between intertial and viscous forces.
How to calculate reynolds number?
To calculate reynolds number you should use the following formula:
Re = (ρ * u * L) / µ
where:
Re = reynolds number
ρ = density of the fluid
u = flow speed
L = characteristic linear dimension
µ = dynamic viscosity of the fluid
Why use reynolds number?
The reynolds number is used in order to get some sense of the relationship in fluid flow between intertial forces.
